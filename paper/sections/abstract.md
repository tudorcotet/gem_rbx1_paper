The GEM × Adaptyv 2026 competition asked the open protein-design
community to make binders against RBX1, the RING-H2 catalytic subunit
of every cullin-RING ubiquitin ligase. 198 teams submitted 12,707
designs; 322 went through expression and SPR at the Adaptyv Foundry. Headline
outcome: 79% (255/322) expressed, 2.8% (9/322) bound, one design
reached the *Strong* bin at KD = 26 nM (`small-vole-maple`, ORBIT,
Mandrake Bio). Mini binders (n=81) dominated the leaderboard;
antibody-family submissions (nanobody n=33, scFv n=10) and peptides
(n=33) populated the long tail. We release the full submission table,
the per-design ESMFold predictions, and the per-replicate SPR
sensorgrams as a public benchmark. We treat the numbers descriptively
— with 9 binders, every per-method or per-modality split is anecdote
territory, not a significance test.
