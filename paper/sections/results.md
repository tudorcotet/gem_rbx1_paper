### Cohort and expression

The 322 designs span 48 teams. `pb_design_class` (ProteinBase modality
call) labels 79 as miniprotein, 33 as nanobody, 32 as peptide, 10 as
scFv, and 164 as *Other* — a bucket that mostly catches de novo
designs the classifier could not bin. 255 of 322 designs expressed
(79%; column `is_expressed` in `data/designs.csv`). Expression rate
varies across method families but with denominators that do not
support a defensible test (`hit_rate_by_method.csv`); we report the
raw counts in [Figure 2A].

### Binding

Nine designs cleared the binder bar (`is_binder` = `binding_strength ∈
{Strong, Medium, Weak}`). One reached *Strong* (KD < 100 nM); seven
landed at *Medium*; one at *Weak*. The Strong design is
`small-vole-maple` (team `aryan-chandak`, method `ORBIT`, a 100-aa
miniprotein), KD = 26 nM (95% CI from replicates: 23–28 nM, n=2 SPR
fits). The next four binders by KD are 85 nM (`deep-bear-clay`,
`kai-yi`, AF2 hallucination + ADFlip), 165 nM (`jade-cobra-cypress`,
same team), 179 nM (`young-panda-rose`, `mike-minson`, ProOS) and 186
nM (`noble-lion-fern`, `guanjiaweitaskin`, method label not provided).
The remaining four sit in the 200–500 nM range. See
[Figure 2C] for the KD ladder.

### Methods

The submitted designs cover at least 12 named method families: the
largest are RFdiffusion (n=27), Mosaic (n=25), BoltzGen (n=22), MoPPIt
(n=20), LFM2 (n=14), and BindCraft 2 (n=7). 93 designs left the method
field blank (`method_family = "Not mentioned"`) and 58 are *Other*
(method label not matched to a known family). Per-method hit rates are
0–25% across the named families. With 1–2 binders per family in the
hits column, none of the per-method differences are defensible as
significant. Pacesa Lab's three binders (`amber-bat-maple`,
`golden-dove-vine`, `rough-eagle-onyx`) were all generated by BindCraft
2 (Pacesa Lab, personal communication).

### Modality

Mini binders dominate the binder set (6/9). Two of Pacesa Lab's
BindCraft 2 hits are labelled as miniprotein, one as nanobody (38 aa,
likely the framework-stripped variant). The single peptide binder is
the Weak hit. No scFv or full nanobody design crossed the bar at the
default-buffer condition.

### Novelty

Six of nine binders sit at < 30% sequence identity to their closest
AFDB50 neighbour (`pb_seqidentity_afdb50`). The three remaining
binders are antibody-derived and closer to known scaffolds (44–62%
identity), consistent with framework-borrowing. The Strong hit is at
21% identity to its closest AFDB50 match.

### Caveats

- 9 binders. The numbers above are descriptive; treat per-method or
  per-modality differences as observations, not claims.
- 79% expression rate refers to the 322-design ProteinBase batch.
- Default-buffer screen only. A second batch in Zn²⁺-loaded buffer
  will be reported separately and may shift outcomes for designs
  targeting the holo state.
- No specificity or developability data is included here; those
  panels will be reported separately.
- Three teams claim binding to the disordered N-terminal tail
  (residues 12–39). There is no published prior art for binding that
  region; treat as a novelty claim AND a specificity concern until the
  Zn²⁺ rerun and specificity panel land.
